SMAD 408 Independent Project Proposal

Professional Portfolio Revamp

Overview

For my independent project, I’m taking my existing professional portfolio and adding interactive elements that showcase my technical skills and better highlight my work experience. By incorporating creative means of showcasing my work, I am both demonstrating what I have learned about interactive and user experience design and promoting the various projects I’ve completed in my undergraduate career. I don’t plan on adding any additional pages to my website, since I think limiting the scope of the site will help avoid overwhelming users. Instead, I’d like to rework some of my existing content so it’s more thorough and advanced.

Goals

My goal is to go beyond a user-friendly site and strive for a user-engaging site, where people are drawn in by my content and want to spend more time looking at my portfolio. I’d like to take the large amount of text I have now and integrate multimedia viewing options and other visual displays of my work so that potential employers or advisors don’t skim through and miss important details. I’d also like to continue doing research on digital portfolio best practices and innovative strategies so my website is on the cutting edge. I want to emphasize to potential employers or advisors that I have the perfect blend of content and context, form and function in my work, and that I know what I’m doing and how to communicate that.

When addressing issues related to Technically Wrong, I’m thinking first and foremost about the accessibility of my website and how I can make the somewhat niche or nuanced projects I’ve done understandable to a more general audience. I might be able to define lingo, add some virtual marginalia, or even include audiovisual explainers that walk people through my work. This will also help me articulate what it is that I do and what I want to do in the future.

Audience

My audience is primarily potential graduate school advisors and faculty and potential employers. I won’t create specific personas representing these individuals, since that often feels like an overgeneralization and might prevent me from seeing some of those “edge cases” Sara Wachter-Boettcher talks about in Technically Wrong. Instead, I’ll just address those cases in writing: at the fringe of potential users for this website are people who might be vetting me to speak at an event, people who meet me at an academic conference and Google search my name, employers at my dream job(s) who stumble on my page through LinkedIn, etc. I need to design my website and work out its advanced technical elements so that these people, almost all of whom have some authority and could benefit me directly, get the best possible impression of me.
